4-6 Breathing

A simple breathing technique that calms your nervous system. Breathe in for 4 seconds, hold for 6 seconds, and exhale for 4 seconds. Repeat to reduce anxiety and find calm.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Reconnect with the present moment using your five senses. Notice 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.

üí° Best for: Panic, dissociation, spiraling thoughts

Kinder Thought Reframing

Challenge negative self-talk by reframing it with compassion. Our mind often exaggerates threats. Learn to speak to yourself like you'd speak to a good friend.

üí° Best for: Self-criticism, perfectionism, negative self-talk

Tips for Using These Tools

  • Practice when calm: Try these techniques when you're not anxious first. This makes them easier to remember when you need them most.
  • Find your favorite: Everyone responds differently. Experiment to find which techniques work best for you.
  • Be patient with yourself: Some tools may feel awkward at first. That's normal. Give yourself time.
  • Combine techniques: Use multiple tools together for a more powerful effect.
  • Talk to a professional: These tools complement (not replace) professional support. If anxiety persists, please reach out to a mental health professional.
